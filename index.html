Across the Canadian province of New Brunswick, the number of people afflicted by a mysterious, potentially deadly brain ailment keeps growing. Neurological symptoms like hallucinations, muscle wasting, vision problems, memory loss, and abnormal movements were seen in 2015 in a small cluster of patients, eventually growing to 48 cases. But some health experts and local residents say the number of people with the condition is much higher — and may exceed 200. The plane — more than 231 feet long with a wingspan of topping 195 feet — is parked at the Freeport A'Famosa Outlet and began boarding shoppers earlier this month. The "Queen of the Skies" took two years to convert into a fully decked-out store.
